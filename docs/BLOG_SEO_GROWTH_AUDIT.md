# Blog SEO Growth Audit

Date: 2026-06-16  
Branch: `feature/blog-seo-growth`

## Current Strengths

- The first article set already covers core buyer questions: what chiptuning is, Stage 1 vs Stage 2, ECU remap safety, AdBlue faults, aftercare and why diagnosis matters before tuning.
- Blog cards are route-backed, not dead cards. The content audit checks that published cards have article pages.
- Article rendering already supports metadata, Article JSON-LD, FAQ JSON-LD, related links, Power Catalog CTA and WhatsApp CTA.
- Dutch is the primary content language, with English and Polish routes already present.
- Customer results are separated from SEO articles, so Facebook-style vehicle posts are not mixed into evergreen content.

## Weakest Areas Before This Pass

- ECU remap did not yet have a dedicated deep article, even though it is a key service and search intent.
- Automatic transmission / DSG / TCU questions were underrepresented.
- Stage 2 content existed, but needed a more practical article about when Stage 2 is actually sensible.
- Loganalyse was present as a service/landing concept, but not fully explained as a pre-tuning decision tool.
- DPF, EGR and AdBlue faults needed a careful combined guide with legal wording and diagnostic-first positioning.
- Article visuals were limited and repeated across content.

## Missing Technical Topics

- ECU remap as software calibration, not just "more power".
- Chiptuning with automatic transmissions, DSG and TCU considerations.
- Stage 2 decision-making: hardware, logs, temperature, maintenance and legal context.
- Log analysis before tuning and during fault diagnosis.
- DSG / TCU tuning as gearbox software, not a generic add-on.
- Responsible DPF / EGR / AdBlue fault diagnosis.

## Missing Local SEO Topics

- Existing Dutch landing pages already cover Assen, Drenthe and Groningen. Blog content can support these pages by linking naturally to chiptuning, auto diagnose, loganalyse and Power Catalog pages.
- Future local content should be based on real work, real customer questions and approved customer results, not thin city pages.

## Missing Service-Supporting Topics

- Automaat/DSG questions support both chiptuning and DSG/TCU service pages.
- Loganalyse content supports auto diagnose, Stage 2 and safe ECU remap.
- Emissions fault content supports diagnostic service pages while keeping public legal wording careful.
- Stage 2 content supports customer result pages and hardware-aware advice.

## Internal Linking Opportunities

- ECU remap article should link to Chiptuning, Auto Diagnose and Power Catalog.
- Automaat article should link to DSG/TCU, Stage 1/Stage 2 context and Power Catalog.
- Stage 2 article should link to Stage 2, Loganalyse and Customer Results.
- Loganalyse article should link to Auto Diagnose and ECU remap safety content.
- DSG/TCU article should link to the automaat article and services.
- DPF/EGR/AdBlue article should link to diagnostics and contact/WhatsApp.

## Power Catalog CTA Opportunities

- Every new article should keep the Power Catalog as the practical next step.
- The catalog should be presented as an indication tool, not as a guarantee.
- WhatsApp should remain the route for final vehicle-specific advice.

## Image / Visual Gaps

- The blog needed more specific visual roles: ECU reading, automatic/OBD work, turbo/hardware, log analysis, DSG/TCU bench work and diagnostics.
- This pass uses only local existing NoordTune assets copied into descriptive blog image paths. No random external images were added.

- Keep Dutch articles strongest and most detailed.
- Keep English and Polish complete, natural and useful.
- Avoid keyword stuffing, exaggerated power claims and illegal emissions promises.
- Keep the tone technical, honest, premium and direct.
